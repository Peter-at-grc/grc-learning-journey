## 1. Introduction — What is GRC?

**GRC** stands for **Governance, Risk, and Compliance** — three interconnected disciplines that together form the backbone of an organisation's security and operational integrity.

| Pillar | Core Definition |
|---|---|
| **Governance** | Establishes the framework, direction, and structure of where the organisation is going |
| **Risk Management** | Identifies, assesses, and prioritises risks to information assets |
| **Compliance** | Ensures the organisation meets external regulatory requirements and internal policies |

### How the Three Pillars Connect

- **Governance** establishes the framework and direction
- **Risk Management** provides the information needed to make good decisions
- **Compliance** ensures that the decisions made meet the required standards

## 4. Tools and Technologies

### GRC Platforms
Large-scale platforms (e.g. **ServiceNow GRC**) act as the *central nervous system* for all GRC activities — tracking risk, policy, and compliance in one place.

### Key Tool Categories

| # | Tool Category | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **GRC Platforms** | Central hub for risk, policy, and compliance tracking |
| 2 | **Risk Assessment Tools** | Maps assets, threat intel, and controls across the environment |
| 3 | **Compliance Management Software** | Maps controls to various frameworks; reduces duplication of effort |
| 4 | **Policy Management** | Creates, distributes, and tracks attestation for policies and procedures |
| 5 | **Vulnerability Management Platforms** | Identifies and tracks technical weaknesses |
| 6 | **Audit Management Tools** | Manages audit workflows and findings |
| 7 | **Data Visualisation & Analytics Tools** | Reporting and dashboards |
| 8 | **Collaboration Tools** | Cross-functional communication |

## 6. GRC Career Pathways

### What Hiring Managers Look For

1. Basic understanding of security concepts
2. Familiarity with at least one compliance framework
3. Strong analytical and documentation skills
4. Attention to detail
5. Communication abilities
6. Willingness to learn

### Career Progression

| Stage | Role | Experience |
|---|---|---|
| Stage 1 | GRC Analyst | 0–3 years |
| Stage 2 | Senior GRC Analyst / Specialist | 3–5 years |
| Stage 3 | GRC Manager | 5–8 years |
| Stage 4 | Director or Executive | 8+ years |

### Alternative Career Paths

1. **Consultancy** (e.g. Big 4, boutique firms)
2. **Specialisation** (e.g. Privacy, Third-Party Risk, Cloud GRC)

## 7. Is GRC Right For Me?

### GRC May NOT Be the Right Fit If You:

1. Prefer hands-on technical work with minimal documentation
2. Dislike meetings and collaborative work environments
3. Find policy and regulatory discussions boring
4. Prefer rapid, tactical responses over strategic, long-term planning

### How to Gain Initial Experience

1. Leverage your current role
2. Pursue relevant certifications — e.g. **CompTIA Security+**, **CISA**
3. Build your knowledge base
4. Create GRC work products (portfolio)
5. Network strategically
6. Consider transitional roles
7. Volunteer

## 8. Reference Diagrams

### NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF 2.0)

The NIST CSF organises cybersecurity activities into six core functions:

```
GOVERN → IDENTIFY → PROTECT → DETECT → RESPOND → RECOVER
```

> These functions form a continuous cycle, not a linear sequence.

### GRC Position Within Cybersecurity (Org Structure)

```
CISO
├── GRC Director
│   ├── Vulnerability Management Analyst
│   └── Risk Analyst
├── SOC Manager
│   └── SOC Analyst
└── Security Tech
    ├── Firewall Engineer
    └── MDM
```

> GRC sits under the CISO alongside SOC and Security Tech — it is a **strategic** function, not just an administrative one.

## 9. Key Takeaways

- GRC is not just paperwork — it is the **decision-making infrastructure** of a secure organisation.
- Governance sets direction; Risk informs decisions; Compliance enforces standards.
- A GRC analyst operates across people, processes, and technology simultaneously.
- The career path is structured and scalable — from Analyst to Executive level.
- Entry does not require deep technical expertise — **documentation, analysis, and communication** are the core skills at entry level.
