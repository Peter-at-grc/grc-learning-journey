# Day 2 — Case Study: Equity Bank Kenya — NIST CSF GOVERN Mapping

> **Source:** Equity Bank Kenya Annual Report 2024 — Cybersecurity Governance Section  
> **Framework:** NIST CSF 2.0 — GOVERN Function (GV)  
> **Date:** 2026-05-13

---

## Objective

Map all 6 NIST CSF GOVERN sub-categories to Equity Bank Kenya's real governance context using publicly available documents. For each sub-category: state what Equity Bank **should** have per NIST CSF, then provide **evidence** of what they actually do based on their 2024 Annual Report.

---

## 1. GV.OC — Organizational Context

**Should:** Equity Bank should document the laws, regulations and business objectives that shape its cybersecurity approach.

**Evidence:** Based on page 20 of the *Equity Bank Kenya Annual Report 2024 — Cybersecurity Governance* section, Equity Bank documents the countries it operates in (Kenya, Tanzania, Rwanda, DR Congo, Uganda and South Sudan), its reach throughout those countries, and how it benchmarks against its peers.

---

## 2. GV.RM — Risk Management Strategy

**Should:** The board should define and document a risk appetite statement that sets limits on acceptable cybersecurity risks.

**Evidence:** Based on page 63, the risk appetite statement defines the aggregate levels and types of risks the group is willing to accept to achieve its objectives. It uses quantitative and qualitative metrics that are monitored and reported.

---

## 3. GV.RR — Roles, Responsibilities and Authorities

**Should:** Define roles explicitly with clear cybersecurity accountability at leadership level.

**Evidence:** As shown on page 37, Equity Bank has a COO, Group Chief Risk Officer, CIO, CISO, and Group Chief Internal Auditor — named leadership roles with defined cybersecurity responsibilities.

---

## 4. GV.PO — Policy

**Should:** A written, board-approved information security policy should exist, be communicated to all staff and be reviewed at least annually.

**Evidence:** Equity Bank has a Risk Management Framework that enables identification, assessment and control of risks within a board-approved risk appetite. It operates a **Three Lines of Defence model:**

| Line | Function |
|---|---|
| **First Line** | Operational Management — owns and manages risks directly through implementing controls, adhering to policies and ensuring regulatory compliance |
| **Second Line** | Risk Management and Compliance functions — provide oversight, frameworks and monitoring to support the first line in identifying, assessing and mitigating risks |
| **Third Line** | Internal Audit — offers independent assurance on the effectiveness of risk management, internal controls and governance, reporting directly to the Board or Audit Committee |

---

## 5. GV.OV — Oversight

**Should:** Have regular meet-ups to receive regular reports on cybersecurity risks.

**Evidence:** The Group Board has 6 Committees that assist it in its responsibilities. In 2024, committees held meetings on a **quarterly basis** in the months of March, May, August and November. Ad hoc meetings were also held to discuss urgent matters.

---

## 6. GV.SC — Supply Chain Risk Management

**Should:** Third-party vendors should be assessed for cybersecurity risk before onboarding and monitored continuously.

**Evidence:** Not clearly defined among the listed risks in the 2024 Annual Report — this represents a **visible gap** in Equity Bank's publicly disclosed governance documentation relative to NIST CSF GV.SC requirements.

---

## Summary Table

| Sub-Category | NIST Requirement | Equity Bank Status |
|---|---|---|
| GV.OC | Document laws, regulations, business objectives | ✅ Documented — multi-country operations and peer benchmarking on p.20 |
| GV.RM | Board-approved risk appetite with defined limits | ✅ Documented — quantitative and qualitative metrics on p.63 |
| GV.RR | Named roles with cybersecurity accountability | ✅ Present — COO, CISO, CIO, Group CRO, Group CIA on p.37 |
| GV.PO | Written, board-approved policy reviewed annually | ✅ Present — Three Lines of Defence model with board-approved risk appetite |
| GV.OV | Regular board-level cybersecurity reporting | ✅ Present — 6 board committees, quarterly meetings in 2024 |
| GV.SC | Third-party vendor cybersecurity risk assessment | ⚠️ Gap — not clearly defined in publicly disclosed documents |

---

## Key Observation

Equity Bank demonstrates strong governance alignment with NIST CSF GOVERN across five of six sub-categories. The one visible gap is **GV.SC** — supply chain and third-party vendor cybersecurity risk management is not clearly articulated in their public disclosures. For a bank operating across six countries with significant technology dependencies, this is a material gap worth noting.
